02 How The Lab Ships

Three phases. No surprises.

Every agent moves through the same path. We don't roll anything to the fleet that hasn't been used by a real recruiter, on a real desk, for a real week.

01

Week 1–2 · Prototype

Prototype

A working agent against real candidate data, scoped to one job. No mocks. We measure what it gets right and what it confidently gets wrong.

  • Real prompts, real data
  • One recruiter in the loop
  • Killable by Friday
02

Week 3–4 · Pilot

Pilot

One recruiter runs the agent every working day. We track time saved, error rate, and the moments they reach for the override. That override log is the spec for v2.

  • Daily usage, not demos
  • Measured in hours back
  • Two stand‑ups per week
03

Week 5+ · Ship

Ship

Roll to the fleet behind a kill‑switch. Co‑Manager picks up monitoring. We keep the override log open for the first 30 days and treat every entry as a bug.

  • Rollouts behind a flag
  • Override log = backlog
  • 30‑day post‑mortem, always

03 Roadmap

From April to June.

P0 was about proving the loop. P1 is about giving the team their inboxes back. P2 is about scaling care past the point a human can.

    Eight agents. The recruiting loop, end to end.

    Outreach, screening, CQ, submittal, interview confirm/prep/feedback, and timesheets. The Digital Twin shipped first because it carries the rest of the brand.

  3. Next · May–June 2026Priority 2 — Scale

    Two agents. Sourcing intelligence and consultant care.

The math is the pitch.

If P0 holds, every recruiter on the floor gets back roughly a working week per month — most of it from outreach, CQ logging, and interview ops. That time goes back into the conversations that actually move the deal.

If P1 holds, Justin's inbox stops being the bottleneck for the company. If P2 holds, we stop hiring against growth.
